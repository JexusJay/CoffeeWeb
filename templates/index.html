Distinct Flavor

Renowned for its bold and earthy taste, Kapeng Barako offers a hint of nuttiness and a unique aroma that sets it apart.

Cultural Heritage

A symbol of Filipino tradition, Kapeng Barako embodies generations of coffee-growing excellence rooted in Batangas and Cavite.

Sustainable Impact

Supporting Kapeng Barako cultivation not only helps local farmers but also preserves biodiversity and the Liberica coffee species.

The Bold Legacy of Kapeng Barako

Kapeng Barako, a variety of Liberica coffee, was introduced to Batangas in the 1740s during the Spanish colonial period and is celebrated for its bold flavor and resilience. It thrived during the 1880s coffee rust crisis, when other coffee varieties failed, cementing its status as a symbol of Filipino heritage. Despite a decline in production in the 20th century, Kapeng Barako remains culturally significant, often enjoyed with traditional Filipino pastries. Recent initiatives, beginning in the early 2000s, focus on revitalizing its cultivation and promoting its unique qualities to a global audience (Source: Philippine Coffee Board).

Fun Fact

Kapeng Barako is one of the rarest coffee varieties, comprising less than 2% of global production.
